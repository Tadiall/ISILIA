# GOVERNANCE

# ISILIA Governance

This document defines how decisions are made within ISILIA.

Its purpose is to maintain:

* clarity
* consistency
* academic quality
* long-term sustainability

As the project grows, this document may evolve.

However, the core principles should remain stable.

---

# Core Principle

ISILIA exists to help learners navigate knowledge.

Every governance decision should support that goal.

When there is uncertainty, contributors should ask:

> Does this improve the learner's ability to learn?

If the answer is no, the change should be reconsidered.

---

# Project Structure

ISILIA is organized around four groups:

```text
Founder
↓
Maintainers
↓
Contributors
↓
Learners
```

As the project grows, additional roles may be introduced.

---

# The Founder

The Founder is responsible for:

* protecting the vision
* protecting academic standards
* protecting the long-term direction
* resolving major disagreements
* approving major structural changes

The Founder is not expected to control every detail.

The Founder serves as the project's steward.

---

# Responsibilities of the Founder

The Founder should:

* explain decisions clearly
* remain open to criticism
* document important changes
* encourage contributions
* avoid becoming a bottleneck

The Founder should not assume they are always right.

Good governance requires listening.

---

# Maintainers

Maintainers are trusted contributors who help manage the project.

Their responsibilities include:

* reviewing contributions
* organizing discussions
* maintaining quality standards
* helping new contributors
* identifying problems

Maintainers help keep the project healthy.

---

# Becoming a Maintainer

A contributor may become a Maintainer after demonstrating:

* consistent participation
* constructive communication
* understanding of the project's goals
* commitment to quality

There is no fixed number of contributions required.

Trust matters more than volume.

---

# Contributors

Contributors are individuals who help improve ISILIA.

Examples:

* curriculum reviewers
* subject matter experts
* developers
* designers
* writers
* educators
* learners providing feedback

Every contribution is valuable when it improves the project.

---

# Learners

Learners are the reason ISILIA exists.

Even learners who never contribute code or documentation provide value through:

* feedback
* questions
* testing learning paths
* reporting confusion

A curriculum that confuses learners needs improvement.

---

# Decision Making

Not all decisions require the same level of review.

---

## Minor Decisions

Examples:

* typo fixes
* formatting improvements
* broken link updates
* documentation clarifications

These can be reviewed and approved by Maintainers.

---

## Medium Decisions

Examples:

* adding resources
* modifying explanations
* adjusting project recommendations

These should be discussed and reviewed before approval.

---

## Major Decisions

Examples:

* creating a new school
* changing curriculum structure
* changing educational philosophy
* changing governance
* changing licensing

These require Founder approval and community discussion.

---

# Disagreements

Disagreement is normal.

It is often useful.

Ideas should be challenged.

Assumptions should be questioned.

Arguments should focus on:

* evidence
* reasoning
* educational value

Not:

* status
* popularity
* authority

---

# Academic Standards

ISILIA prioritizes:

```text
Quality
over
Quantity
```

A smaller curriculum of high-quality resources is preferable to a large curriculum of mediocre resources.

Maintainers and contributors should protect this principle.

---

# Transparency

Important decisions should be documented publicly whenever possible.

Examples:

* roadmap changes
* curriculum changes
* governance updates

Transparency builds trust.

---

# Future Councils

As ISILIA grows, specialized groups may be created.

Examples:

### Academic Council

Responsible for:

* curriculum quality
* educational standards
* resource evaluation

---

### Technical Council

Responsible for:

* platform architecture
* software development
* infrastructure

---

### Community Council

Responsible for:

* contributor onboarding
* community health
* communication

These councils are not required during the early stages of the project.

---

# Conflict of Interest

Contributors should disclose conflicts of interest.

Examples:

* recommending their own paid course
* promoting their own platform
* recommending resources they financially benefit from

Transparency is expected.

---

# Governance Evolution

This governance model is intentionally simple.

Complex structures should only be introduced when necessary.

The goal is not bureaucracy.

The goal is clarity.

---

# Final Principle

ISILIA should remain:

* learner-focused
* transparent
* rigorous
* collaborative

The project belongs neither to algorithms nor to institutions.

It exists to help people learn.

Every decision should support that mission.
