**ISILIA** is an opensource academic compass for autodidacts, curious students, and serious self-learners.

It is not a university.
It is not a bootcamp.
It is not another random list of online courses.

ISILIA exists to help learners answer one simple but difficult question:

> What should I learn, in what order, and why?

---

## Why ISILIA exists

The internet has made knowledge more accessible than ever.

But access is not the same as direction.

A motivated learner can find thousands of courses, books, videos, articles, tutorials, and roadmaps online. The problem is not the lack of resources. The problem is knowing which resources matter, how they connect, and what path to follow without getting lost.

Many self-learners fail not because they are lazy, but because they are alone in a confusing ocean of information.

ISILIA is built to reduce that confusion.

Its purpose is to organize learning paths inspired by serious university-level education, while keeping them free, practical, and accessible.

---

## What ISILIA provides

ISILIA aims to provide:

* clear learning paths
* structured curricula
* prerequisite maps
* recommended resources
* project-based milestones
* study guidance
* progression levels
* contribution-based improvement

The goal is not to replace universities.

The goal is to help people learn with more structure, discipline, and clarity.

---

## Who ISILIA is for

ISILIA is for:

* self-taught learners
* students who want more structure
* people who cannot access expensive education
* developers, builders, researchers, and creators
* anyone who wants a serious long-term learning roadmap
* people who want to study deeply instead of randomly consuming tutorials

ISILIA is especially useful for learners who ask:

* Where do I start?
* What comes after the basics?
* Which courses are worth my time?
* What should I build to prove I understand?
* How do I avoid wasting months jumping between random resources?

---

## What ISILIA is not

ISILIA is not:

* a degree-granting university
* an accredited institution
* a paid course platform
* a shortcut to mastery
* a motivational content project
* a collection of copied course links without structure

ISILIA does not promise certificates, jobs, or instant expertise.

It focuses on direction, structure, and serious learning.

---

## Initial focus

The first version of ISILIA focuses on a small number of fields instead of trying to cover everything immediately.

Initial schools:

```text
schools/
├── computer-science/
└── mathematics/
```

These two areas are chosen first because they form the foundation for many modern fields:

* software engineering
* artificial intelligence
* data science
* cybersecurity
* systems engineering
* scientific computing
* automation

More schools may be added later, but only after the first ones become clear and useful.

* preparing the first public learning paths

---

## Core principle

ISILIA follows a simple principle:

> Do not give learners more noise. Give them direction.

A good curriculum is not just a list of resources.

A good curriculum explains:

* what to learn
* why it matters
* what comes before it
* what comes after it
* how to practice it
* how to prove understanding

---

## How to contribute

Contributions are welcome, but ISILIA values quality over quantity.

Useful contributions include:

* improving curriculum structure
* suggesting better resources
* fixing broken links
* improving explanations
* adding missing prerequisites
* proposing realistic projects
* reviewing learning paths
* improving documentation

Before contributing, read:

```text
CONTRIBUTING.md
GOVERNANCE.md
```
