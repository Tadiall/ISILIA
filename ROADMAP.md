# ISILIA ROADMAP

This roadmap describes the planned evolution of ISILIA.

It is not a promise.

It is a guide.

Plans may change as we learn more, receive feedback, and better understand the needs of learners.

The objective is to build strong foundations before expanding.

---

# Current Status

Project Stage:

```text
Foundation Phase
```

Current priorities:

* define the vision
* define the mission
* establish governance
* create contribution guidelines
* design curriculum architecture
* organize repositories

Before building software, we must understand what we are building and why.

---

# PHASE 3 — THE COMPASS

## Objective

Create the first software platform.

At this stage ISILIA stops being only documentation.

It becomes a usable learning tool.

---

### Deliverables

Web platform:

* user accounts
* roadmap visualization
* progress tracking
* curriculum explorer
* resource browser

---

### Design Principle

The platform should remain simple.

The goal is guidance.

Not social media.

Not content consumption.

Not gamification for its own sake.

---

### Success Criteria

* Working public platform
* Learners actively using pathways
* Progress tracking operational

---

# PHASE 4 — AI ASSISTANCE

## Objective

Introduce AI carefully.

The purpose is assistance.

Not replacement of thinking.

---

### Potential Features

* study planning
* prerequisite guidance
* concept explanation
* project feedback
* curriculum navigation

---

### Restrictions

The AI should:

* guide
* question
* challenge

It should not:

* complete assignments
* write projects
* replace learning effort

---

### Success Criteria

* Useful AI interactions
* Positive learner feedback
* Low dependency on AI-generated answers

---

## Objective

Expand beyond the initial schools.

Potential additions:

* Data Science
* Cybersecurity
* Product Design

* Languages

---

### Success Criteria

* Sustainable contributor base

---

# PHASE 6 — LONG-TERM ECOSYSTEM

## Objective

Become a trusted educational reference.

---

### Possibilities

* mentorship programs
* portfolio review systems
* advanced project tracks
* research pathways
* global translations

---

### Principle

Growth should never reduce quality.

A smaller, high-quality ecosystem is preferable to a larger, low-quality one.

---

# What Will Not Be Prioritized

At least initially, ISILIA will not focus on:

* issuing degrees
* issuing certificates
* ranking learners
* selling courses
* maximizing user counts
* replacing universities

The mission remains educational guidance.

---

# Guiding Rule

When faced with a choice between:

```text
More features
or
Better learning
```

ISILIA chooses:

```text
Better learning
```

Every decision should support that principle.

---

# Looking Ahead

The roadmap will evolve.

The vision may grow.

The technology will change.

The educational landscape will change.

What should remain constant is the commitment to helping learners navigate knowledge with clarity, rigor, and purpose.

That is the direction of ISILIA.
